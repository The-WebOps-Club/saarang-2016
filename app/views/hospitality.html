Upon reaching the IIT Madras campus, participants who registered for accommodation are requested to report to the Control Room, which will be operational day and night (except from 5pm to 12 midnight which is the time for Professional Shows). Participants will be requested to produce all necessary documents at the control room. Nominal charges for accommodation will be collected here depending on the number of days you wish to avail this facility. The control room will close in the afternoon of the final day of Saarang. All participants are required to carry their :

  1. SAAR (Saarang Advanced Accommodation Registration) letter which will be made available to people whose accommodation is confirmed
  2. College identity cards (original and a copy)
  3. Bonafide certificates (original and a copy)

In order to avail accommodation on campus. Participants are also expected to follow certain Rules and Regulations to help us maintain the festive atmosphere in the institute. We would be issuing Saarang ID cards to all the outstation participants.

Outstation participants can reach the IIT Madras campus via bus, auto or call-taxi which is available just outside the station.

A. From Chennai Central

Hire an auto/taxi to IIT Madras..
You can catch a bus just outside the Chennai Central. 18B and 19S are the direct buses to IITM (Get down at Gandhi Mandapam /IITM). From the gate there are IITM buses to the hostels, every 20 minutes.

Hire an auto/taxi to IIT Madras.

C. From Airport:

Hire an auto/taxi to IIT Madras. Inside the campus, get down at the Hospitality Control Room.

Hire an auto/taxi to IIT Madras (inside)

Set in a lush green campus spanning 620 acres in the heart of Chennai, IIT Madras is home to 6000 students and also a few hundred monkeys, deer and blackbucks. Carved from the Guindy National Park, IITM, which celebrated its Golden Jubilee in 2009, is blessed with rich wildlife that perhaps no other college in the world can boast of.

IIT Madras boasts one of the most vibrant student population in India with it's eclectic mix of students taking part in a variety of events on campus ranging from quizzing to hosting and excelling in technical events.

Cultural activities on campus reach its peak in January as Saarang fever takes over the campus. Formerly known as Mardi Gras, Saarang has, over the years, evolved into the behemoth it is today.

IITM being a residential campus has 17 hostels all of which are named after rivers. This along with the fact that the campus buses used to be named after mountains has resulted in an epigram about IIT Madras that it is the only place where the mountains move and the rivers remain still.

Another distinctive feature of the campus is its lingo. A mix of English, Hindi, Telugu, Malayalam and Tamil, aspects of the campus slang has been adopted by some other Chennai colleges. Interestingly enough, the lingo has outgrown the boundaries of the college and has resulted in a Master's thesis about it in a German university.
